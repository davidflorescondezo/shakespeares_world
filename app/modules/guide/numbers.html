Numbers and Currency

Most numbers in early modern documents appear in Roman numerals, although Arabic numerals may also appear in the same documents. The final elongated “i” of some numerals is transcribed as “j.” Be careful not to mistake “x” (Roman numeral 10) as the letter “p.”

Currency abbreviations are also taken from the Latin. “l” or “li” stands for pound (libri), “s” for shilling (solidus), and “d” for pence (denarius).

Roman numerals

  • don’t confuse “x” with “v” (or “p”)
  • be prepared for inconsistency and mixing of forms (xx, xxtie, xxti, xxty)

Arabic numbers

  • “1” often looks like a modern “2”
  • “5” often looks like a modern “s”

Conversions

  • 1 pound = 20 shillings
  • 1 shilling = 12 pence
  • 1 mark = 13 shillings, 4 pence (2/3 of a pound)
